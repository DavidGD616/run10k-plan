05

Race-day execution

Negative split. The first mile should feel almost too easy — that discipline is what produces a fast finish.
MILE 1
8:40–8:43 /mi — deliberately controlled. Let faster starters go. Settle breathing and find rhythm.
MILE 2–4
8:32–8:35 /mi — lock into goal pace. Relaxed shoulders, even effort, tuck behind a group if you can.
MILE 5
8:28–8:31 /mi — begin to press. Re-engage focus, shorten the gap to anyone ahead.
MILE 6
8:22–8:28 /mi — commit. This is where the race is decided. Stay tall and drive the arms.
LAST 0.2
8:00–8:10 /mi pace — empty the tank to the line. Nothing left in reserve.

If you reach mile 4 feeling clearly comfortable, shift the entire back half ~8 s/mi faster and chase the 51:30 stretch goal. The fatal error is going out too fast — guard against it in mile 1.

06

Strength & the 2 leg days

Strength gains take longer than 3 weeks to show — but heavy-leg fatigue absolutely will show on race day. So we maintain, then back off.

Stack it onto hard days

Keep your 2 lower-body sessions on Tue & Thu — the same days as quality running. This consolidates stress into hard days and keeps easy days truly easy. Always lift after the run, or 4+ hours apart.

Ramp the load down

Week 1: moderate–heavy, RIR 2–3. Week 2: cut load ~20–30% & reduce volume; Thursday's is your final lower-body session (light, single-leg/stability). Race week: nothing loading the legs from Tuesday on.

Avoid the soreness traps

No max lifts, no brand-new exercises, and nothing that leaves deep DOMS within the final ~10 days. Skip heavy eccentrics — deep loaded squats to failure, heavy walking lunges — close to race day. Favour compound but submaximal work, calves/feet, and core.

Why this works

In a 3-week window you can't build much new strength, but you can ruin a race by carrying leg fatigue into it. You'll bank the fitness you already have and shed the tiredness — arriving on the line springy, not sore.

07

Coaching notes

The small things that decide the last mile.

Sleep is the #1 taper lever

Aim 7.5–9 hrs, especially Wed–Sat of race week. The night that matters most is two before the race (Friday) — pre-race nerves often disrupt the final night, so bank it early.

Fuel & race morning

Normal balanced eating, with a carb lean (~55–65%) the last 2–3 days. Race morning: a familiar carb-based breakfast 2.5–3 hrs before. Never try anything new on race day.

Carlsbad in June

Coastal "June gloom" often means cool, overcast mornings — excellent racing weather. Check race-morning conditions, keep urine pale through race week, and adjust warm-up layers to the actual temperature.

When to flex the plan

A niggle or unusual fatigue → swap a quality day for easy and trim volume. One missed session won't cost you the race; racing injured will. Sharp pain = stop. Listen to the body over the schedule.

Built from your Strava activity history · paces are targets, effort is the master · adjust the day-of-week layout freely as long as hard days keep a recovery day on either side.
